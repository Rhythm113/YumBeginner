Classic New York Cheesecake

AN INGREDIENT REQUIREMENT PROVIDER

About Classic Cheesecake

The New York-style cheesecake is known for its rich, dense, and creamy texture. This classic version features a buttery graham cracker crust and a smooth, velvety filling made with cream cheese, eggs, and sugar. The secret to its perfect texture is baking it in a water bath, which ensures even cooking and prevents cracking.

This dessert is perfect for special occasions or when you want to impress your guests. While it requires some patience (especially for cooling), the result is absolutely worth it. Serve it plain or with your favorite toppings like fresh berries, fruit compote, or chocolate sauce.

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (160°C). Wrap the outside of a 9-inch springform pan with aluminum foil to prevent water leakage.
  2. For the crust: Mix graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and sugar. Press firmly into the bottom of the pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then let cool.
  3. For the filling: Beat cream cheese until smooth. Gradually add sugar, then eggs one at a time, mixing just until combined.
  4. Add sour cream, vanilla extract, and lemon zest (if using), mixing until just incorporated. Avoid overmixing.
  5. Pour filling over the crust. Place the springform pan in a larger baking pan and fill the outer pan with hot water halfway up the sides of the springform.
  6. Bake for about 1 hour 15 minutes until the edges are set but the center still jiggles slightly.
  7. Turn off the oven, leave the door slightly ajar, and let the cheesecake cool in the oven for 1 hour.
  8. Remove from oven and water bath, then cool completely at room temperature. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, before serving.

Ingredients (for 8 servings):

Graham cracker crumbs
1.5 cups
Unsalted butter
0.33 cup
Sugar (for crust)
0.25 cup
Cream cheese
32 oz
Sugar (for filling)
1 cup
Eggs
4
Sour cream
1 cup
Vanilla extract
2 tsp
Lemon zest
1 tsp
