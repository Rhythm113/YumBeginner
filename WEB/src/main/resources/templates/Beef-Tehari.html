Beef Tehari

AN INGREDIENT REQUIREMENT PROVIDER

About Beef Tehari

Beef Tehari is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish that originates from Bangladesh, featuring tender beef pieces cooked with fragrant spices and rice. This dish is known for its rich taste and beautiful yellow color from the turmeric. The combination of whole spices and mustard oil gives it a distinctive flavor that sets it apart from other rice dishes.

This recipe is perfect for family gatherings or special occasions. The beef becomes incredibly tender when cooked slowly, and the rice absorbs all the wonderful flavors from the spices and meat. You can adjust the spice levels to your preference and serve it with raita or salad for a complete meal.

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pot and fry bay leaf, cinnamon, clove, and cardamom until fragrant.
  2. Add sliced onion and green chili; cook until the onions turn golden brown.
  3. Add beef pieces, garlic-ginger paste, all spices, and salt; cook until the beef is well fried and coated with spices.
  4. Add water and cook the beef on low heat until tender (about 30-40 minutes).
  5. Add washed Chinigura rice and more water if needed (water should be about 1 inch above the rice).
  6. Cover and cook on low heat until the rice is fully cooked and all water is absorbed (about 15-20 minutes).
  7. Let it rest for 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork before serving.
